## Typography

### Primary Typeface

**Modern Sans-Serif** (appears to be similar to Inter, Poppins, or system UI fonts)

#### Headings

- **Font Weight:** Bold (700-800)
- **Style:** Clean, geometric, highly legible
- **Usage:** H1, H2, H3 headers, logo wordmark
- **Characteristics:** Strong, confident, modern

#### Body Text

- **Font Weight:** Regular (400) and Medium (500)
- **Style:** Clean, readable sans-serif
- **Line Height:** Generous spacing for readability
- **Usage:** Paragraphs, descriptions, article content

#### Emphasis Text

- **Font Weight:** Semi-bold (600)
- **Usage:** Subheadings, callouts, important information

### Typography Hierarchy

```
H1 (Hero): 48-64px, Bold, Black
H2 (Sections): 36-48px, Bold, Black
H3 (Subsections): 24-32px, Bold, Black
Body: 16-18px, Regular, Dark Gray/Black
Captions: 14-16px, Regular, Medium Gray
Buttons: 16-18px, Semi-bold, White on Gold
```

### Typography Principles

- High contrast for readability
- Generous line spacing (1.5-1.7)
- Clear hierarchy with size and weight
- Short paragraphs for digital readability
- Sentence case for approachability (not ALL CAPS)

## Brand Do's and Don'ts

### Logo Usage

#### DO:

‚úì Use official logo files
‚úì Maintain minimum clear space around logo
‚úì Use on white, cream, or light backgrounds
‚úì Maintain aspect ratio when scaling
‚úì Use high-resolution files

#### DON'T:

‚úó Stretch, distort, or rotate the logo
‚úó Change logo colors
‚úó Add effects (shadows, glows, outlines)
‚úó Place on busy backgrounds
‚úó Separate hexagon from wordmark in primary logo
‚úó Use low-resolution files

### Color Usage

#### DO:

‚úì Use gold as primary accent color
‚úì Maintain high contrast for text
‚úì Use white space generously
‚úì Apply teal and orange as supporting colors

#### DON'T:

‚úó Use gold on white without sufficient contrast
‚úó Combine too many colors in one design
‚úó Deviate from established color palette
‚úó Use gradients unless subtle and on-brand
